overview

BPM tracker

Mobile app for tracking heart rate

For a UI/UX exercise, I redesigned the heart rate screen of a Fitbit-style app. The brief was to imagine a feature and make it actually good from a UX standpoint. I focused on one thing: making BPM data readable in real time, not just after the fact.

Role UX / UI Design - solo exercise
Type Feature design, mobile app
Platform iOS, Android
Focus Clarity, real-time feedback, health data

the problem

A lot of tracking app show a full-day line chart. The zones exist (Fat Burn, Cardio, Peak) but they only appear as color on the spikes. Outside of that, the chart is flat and contextless. There's no quick way to know where you currently are or whether it matters. Opening the app mid-workout means scanning through 24 hours of data to find the last few minutes. That's not how people use it.

the solution

I kept the 24h trend as a reference, but made the current hour the main focus. Bar charts per hour, a clear BPM reading up top, and a color-coded legend that's always visible. You open the app and immediately know what's going on, no digging required.
