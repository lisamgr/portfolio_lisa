overview

BPM Tracker

Mobile app for tracking heart rate

For a UI/UX exercise, I redesigned the heart rate screen of a fitness tracking app. The goal was to make BPM data actually readable in real time, not just as a post-workout archive.

Role UX / UI Design, solo exercise
Type Feature design, mobile app
Platform iOS, Android
Focus Clarity, real-time feedback, health data

research

I audited several existing fitness apps. The pattern was consistent: a full-day line chart with no immediate context. Heart rate zones exist but only appear as color accents on spikes. Outside of those moments, the chart is flat and hard to read at a glance. There is no clear answer to "how am I doing right now?"

the problem

Data without context

Opening the app mid-workout means scanning 24 hours of data to find the last few minutes. The zones are there, but you have to know where to look. That is not how people actually use a heart rate monitor.

the solution

Context at a glance

Bar charts per hour for a quick read of recent activity. Current BPM prominently at the top. Color-coded zones always visible, not just on spikes. Overview first, detail on demand.

reflection

The main challenge was balancing information density with clarity. Health data can get overwhelming fast. The key insight: people check their heart rate in specific moments, mid-workout, after an effort, at rest. The design had to answer that question immediately.
